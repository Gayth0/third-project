types of photography

Underwater photography

Underwater photography is the process of taking pictures underwater, usually by diving or swimming in water, and this type of photography is a great challenge because it requires very specialized equipment and techniques. Underwater photography is the creative art of taking pictures underwater, and this is the challenge in that you are doing two different things at the same time. You combine mastery of diving or swimming with the skill of using an underwater camera.

Natural landscape

In photographing nature, the presence of land, sky and water, if any, must be taken into account. Mixing these natural formations with the presence of the moon or stars at a certain angle and lighting gives the photographer impressive results.. The best times to photograph landscapes are sunset or sunrise with the presence of clouds and observing their movement and shadows.

Night photography

Night photography has a specific time, and the ideal time for shooting is minutes after sunset or before sunrise when the sky is dark blue and the flash is not used in night photos and we need a tripod for the camera or we put the camera on a stable surface. To ensure that the camera does not shake.

Macro photography

Close-up photography is photographing close-up objects while showing their fine details such as insects, flowers, book pages, etc. (types of close-up photography) Close-up depiction with a stereo ratio of 2:1 Macro photography with a stereo ratio of (1:1) - it is the normal size - Micro photography, with a stereo ratio of (1:10) It is entering into the very minute details is everything you do not see with the naked eye.
